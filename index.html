Yug Ajmera

Roboticist

Hi!

I am a senior in mechanical engineering at BITS Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, India. I focus on developing versatile robotic systems that can learn multiple tasks in complex environments and collaborate effectively with humans and other agents through interaction. My research interests lie at the conjunction of Machine Learning and Robotics, known as Robot Learning.

I am currently pursuing my undergraduate thesis co-supervised by Prof. Jan Peters and Prof. Ruth Stock-Homburg, on learning robot motions to achieve human-like handshake interaction with one-of-a-kind android robot, Elenoide. In the past, I've had the good fortune of working closely with some wonderful researchers such as Prof. Amir Ghalamzan Esfahani, Prof. Arshad Javed, Prof. Paresh Saxena and Prof. Sudeepta Mishra. I have also led the software team of Hyperloop India to participate in the Hyperloop pod competition organized by SpaceX.

Do reach out to me if you feel my work align with your personal/organizational goals. I am always on the lookout for meaningful opportunities and collaborations.

Past Projects

  • Autonomous UAV framework for search and rescue mission: Applied approximated Q-learning algorithm with a PID position controller to search for a target in an unknown cluttered environment. Uniquely combined YOLO with Optical Flow to track and follow the target victim until rescue.

  • Biped-humanoid robot: Designed the robot on Solidworks, integrated the electronics, fabricated and assembled it. Developed a static walking algorithm and validated it on the robot. [code] [video]

  • Smart spoon: Fabricated a 3D printed low-cost spoon for people with hand tremors. Used an inertial measurement unit(IMU) to sense tremors and applied a PID control algorithm to keep the spoon stable.

  • Line Maze solver using OpenCV & ROS: Implemented multiple centroid tracking algorithm to follow lines and used the left-hand rule to solve the maze. The results of the algorithm were verified using Gazebo simulations. [code]

  • Hexacopter: Successfully built a Hexarotor based on the pixhawk flight controller equipped with GPS and telemetry modules. [link]

  • Robotic arm: Created custom urdf model of a robot manipulator in gazebo and performed motion planning using Moveit and python scripts. [code]

  • Voice Controlled Car: Built a car that can be controlled using specific voice commands. It has about 50K+ views on Arudino.cc. [link]

  • Visual navigation for quadrotor: Implemented Monocular SLAM using PTAM (Parallel Tracking and Mapping) on a Gazebo simulation of a quadrotor. [code]

  • Radar using Processing 3: Object detection system that measures the distance from the surroundings and shows it on screen using the Processing 3 software. [link]

  • Mobile Controlled Car: Developed a mobile robot that can be controlled by a mobile phone using an android application. [link]

  • Secret Clap door lock: Built arduino based system that hears the pattern of claps and compares it with the saved pattern. If both matches, the lock opens. [link]

  • Gesture controlled car: Created a mobile robot that can be controlled by human hand gestures. Tilt of the hand determines the direction of the movement of the car. [link]
